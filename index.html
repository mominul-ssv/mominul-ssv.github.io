A little about me: I'm a Graduate Teaching Assistant (GTA) and currently pursuing an MSc in CS at North South University (NSU). I have three years of teaching experience as a Lecturer, Lab Instructor, and Teaching Assistant, and I’ve taught/mentored over 1,200 students. I am also a collaborator at the NSU Center for Quantum Computing (NCQC) and have published as the first author in Q1 journals, including Computers in Biology and Medicine and PLOS One.


Research Interests:

Biomedical AI & Vision: Data-efficient medical imaging and clinical reasoning using semi-supervised learning, GANs,diffusion models, segmentation; exploring GNNs and digital twins for drug discovery and treatment simulation.

Generative & Quantum ML: Transformer-based models for multilingual/domain-specific tasks with instruction tuning and PEFT; interests in VLMs, RL, reward modeling, uncertainty-aware reasoning, and hybrid classical–quantum architectures.

AI & Human-Centered Research:Agentic decision-making in generative AI, trustworthy reasoning via RAG, with broader focus on fairness, accessibility, and embedding cultural values in AI.
